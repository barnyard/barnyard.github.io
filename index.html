What is (was) Pi?

The Pi project was an effort to create a massively scalable application substrate for the development of Cloud and other applications and services, driven by the need to support a proprietary cloud platform consisting of many thousands of machines providing compute, storage and network services.

A fully functional, API-compatible clones of Amazon's EC2 and S3 services were created and released in a 'private cloud' scenario.

Status

Shortly after Pi won the internal award for Most Innovative Software Product in early 2011, strategic realignment and organisational seismics put a stop to the development effort. Although Pi remains in use, it is effectively on life support.

The codebase was open-sourced in 2012 in an effort to extend its lifespan. It is also our desire to preserve and share the successes and learnings from developing and deploying a platform with a demanding set of infrequently encountered design goals.

About this site

The software has been in production use since 2011, however given its status you are advised to look at currently active projects such as OpenStack.

At this stage, the open-sourced software stack is most likely to be useful as a reference for anyone with an interest in or looking to build highly scalable infrastructure software services.
